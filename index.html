What is the process for requesting an automation or integration ?

You can contact me through the website or via email to discuss your specific needs. From there, we will scope your needs and I will provide you with a quote for the project or discuss subscription options if you're interested in ongoing work. How fast are integrations and automations implemented ?

This will vary greatly depending on the complexity of what you're trying to achieve. Regular automations can be implemented within 2-3 business days, but building app with multiple complex workflows can take much longer. This would be specified on the quote and will be communicated to you beforehand.

Can solutions be done with just one tool, or do I have to use both Make and Airtable ?

Yes, it's possible to create solutions with just one tool, depending on the complexity of your needs. Make and Airtable are powerful tools that can help you store data and automate your workflows, but each has its own strengths and weaknesses. Airtable is ideal for managing data and creating relational databases, while Make is great for automating tasks and integrating with external applications. If you have a simple task or process that needs to be automated, you may be able to accomplish it with just one tool. However, for more complex workflows that involve managing data and automating tasks, you may need to use both Airtable and Make.

What does each tool do ?

Airtable is a cloud-based database management system that allows you to create custom relational databases, manage data, and collaborate with team members. It has a user-friendly interface and is highly customizable, making it a great tool for managing various types of data.
Make is a no-code automation tool that allows you to build custom workflows and automate tasks between different applications. With Make, you can integrate various apps, create custom scenarios, and automate repetitive tasks. It also has a visual interface that makes it easy to create and manage your workflows.

Can you work with other platforms besides Webflow, Airtable, and Make ?

Of course, and that will depend on your exact needs. Membership websites will require gating solutions like Wized, Memberstack or Outseta, and some web-apps will require more complex back-end solutions, like Xano or Firebase. While those tools are my primary areas of expertise, I'm open to exploring other platforms depending on the specific needs of your project. Feel free to reach out and we can discuss your options.
